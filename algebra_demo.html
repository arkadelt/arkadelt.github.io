Solve \(\displaystyle \left(x - 3 \right)\left( x - 1 \right) = 2x + 5\)
We expand the left-hand-side:
\[ \begin{align} \left(x - 3 \right)\left( x - 1 \right) & = 2x + 5 \\ x\left( x - 1 \right) - 3\left( x - 1 \right) & = 2x + 5 \\ x^2 - x - 3x + 3 & = 2x + 5 \end{align}\]
\[x^2 - 4x + 3 = 2x + 5\]
We collect the terms on the left-hand-side:
\[ \begin{align} x^2 - 4x + 3 & = 2x + 5 \\ x^2 - 4x + 3 - 5 & = 2x + 5 - 5 \\ x^2 - 4x - 2 & = 2x \\ x^2 - 4x - 2 - 2x & = 2x - 2x \end{align}\]
\[x^2 - 6x - 2 = 0\]
We complete the square and simplify:
\[ \begin{align} x^2 - 6x - 2 & = 0 \\ (x - 3)^2 - 9 - 2 & = 0 \\ (x - 3)^2 - 11 & = 0 \\ (x - 3)^2 & = 11 \\ x - 3 & = \pm \sqrt{11} \end{align}\]
\[x = 3 \pm \sqrt{11}\]
