If the solution sets of the equations \(8x + 6 = 38\) and \(ax - 18 = a\) in \(\mathbb Q\) are equal, find the value of \(a\).
The solution set of \(8x + 6 = 38\) is \(\{4\}\).
We apply the same operations to both sides of \(8x + 6 = 38\) to find its solution set. \[\begin{align} 8x + 6 & = 38 \\ 8x & = 32 \\ x & = 4\text. \end{align}\] Hence the solution set of \(8x + 6 = 38\) is \(\{4\}\).
The solution set of \(ax - 18 = a\) is \(\left\{\frac{a+18}{a}\right\}\).
We apply the same operations to both sides of \(ax - 18 = a\) to find its solution set. \[\begin{align} ax - 18 & = a \\ ax & = a + 18 \\ x & = \frac{a + 18}{a}\text. \end{align}\] Hence the solution set of \(ax - 18 = a\) is \(\left\{\frac{a+18}{a}\right\}\).
These solution sets are equal, hence \(4 = \frac{a+18}{a}\) and so \[a = 6\text.\]
The two solution sets are equal: \[\{4\} = \left\{\frac{a+18}{a}\right\} \text.\] Hence, their elements are equal: \[4 = \frac{a+18}{a} \text.\] We solve this equation for \(a\): \[\begin{align} 4 & = \frac{a+18}{a} \\ 4a & = a + 18 \\ 3a & = 18 \\ a & = 6\text. \end{align}\]
