Find algebraically the solution set of \(4\left|6 - x\right| - \sqrt{x^2 - 12x + 36} = 15\) in \(\mathbb R\).
We first note that \[\begin{align} \sqrt{x^2 - 12x + 36} & = \sqrt{(6 - x)^2} \\ & = \left|6 - x\right|\text. \end{align}\]
Factorising \(x^2 - 12x + 36\) gives \((x - 6)^2\), hence \[\sqrt{x^2 - 12x + 36} = \sqrt{(x - 6)^2} \text.\] As \(\sqrt{a^2} = \left|a\right|\), \[\sqrt{x^2 - 12x + 36} = \left|x - 6\right| \text.\] As \(\left|a\right| = \left|-a\right|\), \[\begin{align} \sqrt{x^2 - 12x + 36} & = \left|- (x - 6)\right| \\ & = \left|6 - x\right|\text. \end{align}\]
Substituting \(\left|6 - x\right|\) for \(\sqrt{x^2 - 12x + 36}\) in the equation and simplifying, we get \[\left|6 - x\right| = 5 \text.\]
Substituting \(\left|6 - x\right|\) for \(\sqrt{x^2 - 12x + 36}\) in the equation and simplifying, we get \[\begin{align} 4\left|6 - x\right| - \sqrt{x^2 - 12x + 36} & = 15 \\ 4\left|6 - x\right| - \left|6 - x\right| & = 15 \\ 3\left|6 - x\right| & = 15 \\ \left|6 - x\right| & = 5 \text. \end{align}\]
Considering the cases \(x \geq 6\) and \(x < 6\) separately, we find the solution set is \[\{1, 11\}\text.\]
    Case 1: When \(x \geq 6\), \(\left|6 - x\right| = x - 6\). Therefore, \[\begin{align} \left|6 - x\right| & = 5 \\ x - 6 & = 5 \\ x & = 11 \text. \end{align}\]
    Case 2: When \(x < 6\), \(\left|6 - x\right| = 6 - x\). Therefore, \[\begin{align} \left|6 - x\right| & = 5 \\ 6 - x & = 5 \\ x & = 1 \text. \end{align}\]
Therefore, the solution set of the equation is \(\{1, 11\}\).
