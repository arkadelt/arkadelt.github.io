Find the value of \(x\) in \(-6x^2 - 30x - 36 = 0\).
We factorise the left-hand side to obtain \[-6(x+2)(x+3) = 0 \text.\]
We first take out a constant factor of \(-6\): \[\begin{align} -6x^2 - 30x - 36 & = \color{red}{(-6)}\color{black}x^2 + \color{red}{(-6)}\color{black}5x + \color{red}{(-6)}\color{black}6 \\ & = \color{red}{(-6)}\color{black}\left(x^2 + 5x + 6\right) \text. \end{align}\]
Now we factorise the algebraic part: \[\begin{align} x^2 + 5x + 6 & = x^2 + (3 + 2)x + 6 \\ & = x^2 + 3x + 2x + 6 \\ & = x(x + 3) + 2(x + 3) \\ & = (x + 2)(x + 3) \text. \end{align}\]
To factorise \(x^2 + 5x + 6\) we need to find integers \(a\) and \(b\) such that \(a + b = 5\) and \(a\times b = 6\), then we will have: \[\begin{align} x^2 + 5x + 6 & = x^2 + (a + b)x + a\times b \\ & = (x + a)(x + b)\text. \end{align}\]

We do this by writing down all the factor pairs of \(6\), and seeing which pair adds to \(5\):

\(a\) \(b\) \(a + b\)
\(-6\) \(-1\) \(-7\)
\(-3\) \(-2\) \(-5\)
\(1\) \(6\) \(7\)
\(2\) \(3\) \(5\)
Hence \(a = 2\), \(b = 3\), and \[x^2 + 5x + 6 = (x + 2)(x + 3) \text.\]
Substituting this into the equation, we get \[-6(x+2)(x+3) = 0 \text.\]
Therefore, the possible values of \(x\) are \[x = -2 \text{ and } x = -3 \text.\]
For \(-6(x+2)(x+3)\) to equal zero, one of its factors must equal zero. Hence, either \[\begin{align}-6 & = 0 \text{,} \\ x + 2 & = 0 \text{, or} \\ x + 3 & = 0 \text. \end{align}\]

The first factor, \(-6\), cannot equal zero.

For the second factor:

\[\begin{align} x + 2 & = 0 \\ x & = -2\text. \end{align}\]

For the third factor:

\[\begin{align} x + 3 & = 0 \\ x & = -3\text. \end{align}\] Therefore the possible values of \(x\) are \(-2\) and \(-3\).
