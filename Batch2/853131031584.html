Without solving the equation \((7x + 2)(8x + 1) = 0\), find the sum of its roots.
Recall that the sum of the roots of \(ax^2 + bx + c = 0\) is \(-\frac{b}{a}\).
By the quadratic formula, the roots of \[ax^2 + bx + c = 0\] are \[\{L, M\} = \left\{\frac{-b-\sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a}, \frac{-b+\sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a}\right\}\text.\] Hence, the sum of the roots is \[\begin{align} L + M & = \frac{-b - \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a} + \frac{-b + \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a} \\ & = \frac{-b - \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac} - b + \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a} \\ & = \frac{-2b}{2a} \\ & = -\frac{b}{a} \text. \end{align}\]
We can expand the left-hand side of the equation to get: \[56x^2 + 23x + 2 = 0 \text.\]
We expand the left-hand side, to get: \[\begin{align} (7x + 2)(8x + 1) & = 7x(8x + 1) + 2(8x + 1) \\ & = 56x^2 + 7x + 16x + 2 \\ & = 56x^2 + 23x + 2 \text. \end{align}\] Hence the equation \((7x + 2)(8x + 1) = 0\) can be rewritten as \[56x^2 + 23x + 2 = 0 \text.\]
Hence the sum of the roots of the equation is \[-\frac{b}{a} = -\frac{23}{56} \text.\]
