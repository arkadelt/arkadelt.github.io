Find the solution set of the inequality \(\frac{6x-27}{4} \geq \frac{4}{5} \) in \(\mathbb{Q}\).
We multiply both sides by \(4\):
\[ \begin{align} \frac{6x - 27}{4} & \geq \frac{4}{5} \\ 4\times\frac{6x - 27}{4} & \geq 4\times \frac{4}{5} \\ 6x - 27 & \geq \frac{16}{5} \text{.} \end{align} \] Note that as \(4\) is positive we didn't have to flip the inequality sign.
\[6x - 27 \geq \frac{16}{5}\]
We add \(27\) to both sides to move all the constant terms to the right-hand side:
\[ \begin{align} 6x - 27 & \geq \frac{16}{5} \\ 6x & \geq \frac{16}{5} + 27\\ 6x & \geq \frac{16}{5} + \frac{135}{5} \\ 6x & \geq \frac{151}{5} \text{.} \end{align} \]
\[6x \geq \frac{151}{5}\]
We divide both sides by \(6\) to isolate \(x\) on the left-hand side:
\[ \begin{align} 6x & \geq \frac{151}{5} \\ 6x \div 6 & \geq \frac{151}{5} \div 6\\ x & \geq \frac{151}{30} \text{.} \end{align} \]
\[x \geq \frac{151}{30}\]
So the solution set is \(\displaystyle \left\{x : x \in \mathbb{Q}, x \geq \frac{151}{30} \right\}\).
