Find the equation of the straight line passing through the point \(A(-1, 3)\) and the point of intersection of the two lines whose equations are \(3x - y + 5 = 0\) and \(5x + 2y + 3 = 0\).
We solve the simultaneous equations to find the coordinates of the intersection point of the lines, \(\left(-\frac{13}{11}, \frac{16}{11}\right)\).
We want to find the coordinates of the point of intersection of \(3x - y + 5 = 0\) and \(5x + 2y + 3 = 0\).
First, we eliminate \(y\) from the equations to find the \(x\)-coordinate of the intersection, \(-\frac{13}{11}\).
We multiply both sides of the first equation by \(2\), to obtain \[6x - 2y + 10 = 0 \text.\] We add this to to second equation, to get: \[5x + 2y + 3 + 6x - 2y + 10 = 0 + 0 \text.\] We collect like terms and rearrange for \(x\), and get: \[ \begin{align} 5x + 2y + 3 + 6x - 2y + 10 & = 0 + 0 \\ 11x + 0y + 13 & = 0 \\ 11x & = -13 \\ x & = -\frac{13}{11} \text. \end{align} \]
Then, we substitute this value into one of the equations to find the \(y\)-coordinate, \(\frac{16}{11}\).
We substitute the value of the \(x\)-coordinate, \(-\frac{13}{11}\), into one of the equations to find the \(y\)-coordinate. \[ \begin{align} 3x - y + 5 & = 0 \\ 3\times\left(-\frac{13}{11}\right) - y + 5 & = 0 \\ -\frac{39}{11} - y + 5 & = 0 \\ \frac{16}{11} - y & = 0 \\ y & = \frac{16}{11} \text. \end{align} \]

Hence the coordinates of the point of intersection of the lines are \(\left(-\frac{13}{11}, \frac{16}{11}\right)\).

The equation of the line through the given point \(A(-1, 3)\) and the intersection point \(\left(-\frac{13}{11}, \frac{16}{11}\right)\) is \[ 17x - 2y + 23 = 0 \text.\]
In general, the equation of the line through \((x_1, y_1)\) and \((x_2, y_2)\) is \[y - y_1 = \frac{y_2 - y_1}{x_2 - x_1}\left(x - x_1\right)\] Substituting in \((-1, 3)\) and \(\left(-\frac{13}{11}, \frac{16}{11}\right)\) for \((x_1, y_1)\) and \((x_2, y_2)\), we get: \[ \begin{align} y - 3 & = \frac{\frac{16}{11} - 3}{-\frac{13}{11} - (-1)}(x -(-1)) \\ y - 3 & = \frac{16 - 33}{-13 - (-11)}(x + 1) \\ y - 3 & = \frac{17}{2}(x + 1) \\ 2y - 6 & = 17(x + 1) \\ 17x - 2y + 23 & = 0 \text. \end{align} \]
