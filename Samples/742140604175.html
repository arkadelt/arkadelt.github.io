In the following figure, \(AC=CD\), \(AB=BD\), \(m\angle ABC=81^\circ\), and \(m\angle A=43^\circ\). Find \(m\angle ACD\).
As the sum of a triangle's angle measures is \(180^\circ\),
\[ \begin{align} m\angle ACB + m\angle CBA + m\angle BAC & = 180^\circ \\ m\angle ACB + 81^\circ + 43^\circ & = 180^\circ \\ m\angle ACB + 124^\circ & = 180^\circ \\ m\angle ACB & = 180^\circ - 124^\circ \\ m\angle ACB & = 56^\circ \text{.} \end{align}\]
\[m\angle ACB = 56^\circ \text{.}\]

Consider \(\triangle ABC\) and \(\triangle DBC\).

\(AB = BD\), \(AC = CD\) and \(\overline{BC}\) is common to both triangles.

Hence, by the SSS criterion for congruence, \(\triangle ABC \equiv \triangle DBC\).

\[\text{By the SSS criterion for congruence, }\triangle ABC \equiv \triangle DBC \text.\]

\(\angle BCD\) and \(\angle ACB\) are corresponding angles of congruent triangles.

Hence \[ \begin{align} m\angle BCD &= m\angle ACB \\ &= 56^\circ \text. \end{align}\]
\[\text{Hence } m\angle BCD = 56^\circ \text.\]
Putting these results together, we get
\[ \begin{align} m\angle ACD & = m\angle ACB + m\angle BCD \\ & = 56^\circ + 56^\circ \\ & = 112^\circ \text{.} \end{align}\]
\[m\angle ACD = 112^\circ \text{.}\]
