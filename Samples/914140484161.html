Find the area of the gray part.
The area of the gray part is equal to the difference of the areas of the outer rectangle and the inner square.
The area of the inner square is
\[ \begin{align} \text{Area} & = \text{sidelength} \times \text{sidelength} \\ & = 24 \text{ cm} \times 24 \text{ cm}\\ & = 576 \text{ cm}^2 \text. \end{align}\]
\[576 \text{ cm}^2 \text{.}\]
The area of the outer rectangle is
\[ \begin{align} \text{Area} & = \text{length} \times \text{width} \\ & = 78 \text{ cm} \times 64 \text{ cm}\\ & = 4992 \text{ cm}^2 \text. \end{align}\]
\[4992 \text{ cm}^2 \text{.}\]
Hence, the area of the gray part is
\[ \begin{align} \text{Area of gray part} & = \text{Area of outer rectangle} - \text{Area of inner square} \\ & = 4992 \text{ cm}^2 - 576 \text{ cm}^2 \\ & = 4416 \text{ cm}^2 \text. \end{align}\]
\[4416 \text{ cm}^2 \text{.}\]
