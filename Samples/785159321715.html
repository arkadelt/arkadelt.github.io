In a triangle \(ABC\), \(a = 18 \text{ cm}\), \(b = 10 \text{ cm}\), and \(m \angle C = 76^\circ\). Find \(m \angle A\) approximated to the nearest second.
We apply the cosine rule to find that \(c = 18.35505\ldots \text{ cm}\).
We are given the lengths of two sides and the measure of the included angle, and so the only thing we can do is to apply the cosine rule to find \(c\): \[\begin{align} c^2 & = a^2 + b^2 - 2ab\cos C \\ & = 18^2 + 10^2 - 2\times18\times10\times\cos{76^\circ} \\ & = 324 + 100 - 360\cos{76^\circ} \\ & = 336.908117\ldots \\ \end{align}\] Hence, \[\begin{align} c & = \sqrt{336.90811\ldots} \\ & = 18.35505\ldots \text{ cm.} \end{align}\]
We apply the sine rule to sides \(a\) and \(c\), to find that \(m\angle A \approx 72^\circ5'14''\).
Now that we know the lengths of all three sides, we could apply the cosine rule to find \(m\angle A\). However, it is easier to apply the sine rule.

Rearranging \(\frac{\sin{A}}{a} = \frac{\sin{C}}{c}\) and substituting the known values, we get:

\[\begin{align} \sin{A} & = a\frac{\sin{C}}{c} \\ & = 18\frac{\sin{76^\circ}}{18.35505\ldots} \\ & = 0.95152\ldots \end{align}\] Hence, \[\begin{align} m\angle{A} & = \sin^{-1} (0.95152\ldots) \\ & = 72^\circ5'14'' \text. \end{align}\]
