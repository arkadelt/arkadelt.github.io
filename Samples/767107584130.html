Determine the global maximum and minimum values of the function \(f(x) = \begin{cases} (x + 8)^2 & \text{for } x < -1 \\ (x - 7)^2 & \text{for } x \geq -1 \end{cases}\) over the interval \([-3, 5]\).
We split the interval \([-3, 5]\) into the intervals \([-3, -1)\) and \([-1, 5]\) and consider them separately:
The minimum value of \(f(x)\) on the interval \([-3, -1)\) is \(25\), and the maximum (unattained) is \(49\).

In this interval, \(f(x) = (x + 8)^2\).

The values at the endpoints of the interval \([-3, -1)\) are \[f(-3) = 25 \text{ (attained) and } \lim_{x \to -1^-} f(x) = 49 \text{ (not attained).}\]
At the lower endpoint, when \(x = -3\): \[ \begin{align} f(-3) & = ((-3) + 8)^2 \\ & = 5^2 \\ & = 25 \text. \end{align} \] As the interval is right-open, for the upper endpoint we evaluate: \[ \begin{align} \lim_{x \to -1^-} f(x) & = \lim_{x \to -1^-} (x + 8)^2 \\ & = ((-1) + 8)^2 \\ & = 7^2 \\ & = 49 \text. \end{align} \] Note that this value is not attained on the interval \([-3, -1)\), but any value suitably close will be.
There are no critical points in the interval \([-3, -1)\), so the function is monotonic on this interval.
To do this, we find must find where the derivative is zero.

In this interval,

\[ \begin{align} f'(x) & = \frac{d}{dx} (x + 8)^2 \\ & = 2(x + 8) \text. \end{align} \] The critical values of \(x\) are where \[ \begin{align} f'(x) & = 0 \\ 2(x + 8) & = 0 \\ x + 8 & = 0 \\ x & = -8 \text. \end{align} \] As this lies outside the interval \([-3, -1)\), there are no critical points in this interval, and the function is monotonic on this interval.

Hence the minimum value of \(f(x)\) on this interval is \(25\), and the maximum (unattained) is \(49\).
